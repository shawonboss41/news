Barcelona have agreed a deal to sign Ilkay Gundogan from Manchester City on a free transfer, with the midfielder set to pen a three-year contract.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The move has not been officially announced by either side but City have adopted a pragmatic approach due to the player’s age and length of contract the 32-year-old was seeking, in keeping with similar situations over recent years.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Gundogan was in talks with City over a new deal, with his contract set to expire at the end of June.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

However, the length of the deal on offer for the 32-year-old – one year plus an option of a further 12 months – was a sticking point. Arsenal were are also interested in Gundogan.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The influential former Borussia Dortmund midfielder has won five Premier League titles, two FA Cups, four League Cups and the Champions League in his seven years at City.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Gundogan lifted three trophies in the 2022\/23 season as City became only the second English side to win the treble.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The fee is understood to be worth an initial \u00a325m guaranteed plus \u00a35m in performance-related add-ons. A formal announcement of Kovacic joining last season’s treble winners is expected soon.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
